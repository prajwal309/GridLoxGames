Bagchal - The Game of Tigers and Goats

About the Game

Bagchal, a traditional Nepali board game, is a strategy game where players take the role of either tigers or goats. The objective of the game varies depending on the role. The game represents a battle between tigers (the predators) and goats (the prey) on a five-pointed grid resembling a cross.

Rules of the Game

  • Bagchal is played on a 5x5 point board, with 4 tigers and 20 goats.
  • The goal of the tigers is to "capture" 5 goats by jumping over them.
  • The goal of the goats is to trap the tigers, making it impossible for them to move.
  • Players take turns: one player controls the tigers, and the other controls the goats.
  • The tigers start at the four corners of the board. The goats start off the board.
  • The goat player places one goat on the board per turn, while the tiger player can move one tiger per turn.
  • Once all goats are placed, they can move to adjacent points along the lines on the board.
  • A tiger captures a goat by jumping over it to an adjacent vacant point.
  • Tigers cannot jump over other tigers, and goats cannot jump over anything.
  • The game ends when the tigers capture 5 goats or when all tigers are trapped and unable to move.
